W0010 — 这

1 🔊

Pronunciation

这 Pinyin: zhè (or zhèi before measure words) Tone: Fourth tone (falling) Meaning: this; these

Before a measure word, 这 is often pronounced zhèi in natural speech (though zhè is always correct in writing):

这个 → zhège or zhèige

2 📖

Core Usage

这 points to something near the speaker. It pairs with 那 (nà — that), which points to something farther away.

这 + Measure Word + Noun → this [noun] 这 + 是 → this is... 这里 / 这儿 → here

3 ✎

Examples

这是什么？ Zhè shì shénme? What is this? Literal: This / is / what?

这个很好。 Zhège hěn hǎo. This one is very good. Literal: This / [measure word] / very / good.

这里有人吗？ Zhèlǐ yǒu rén ma? Is anyone here? Literal: Here / have / person?

4 ？

Question

这是你的吗？ Zhè shì nǐ de ma? Is this yours?

✅ 是的，这是我的。 Shì de, zhè shì wǒ de. Yes, this is mine.

❌ 不，这不是我的。 Bù, zhè bú shì wǒ de. No, this isn't mine.

5 ❖

这 vs 那

这 — this (near speaker) 那 (nà) — that (away from speaker)

这个人 — this person (here) 那个人 — that person (there)

6 ⚠

Common Mistake

这 cannot stand alone before a noun in most contexts — a measure word is usually required:

❌ 这书 (unnatural in standard speech) ✅ 这本书 (this book — 本 is the measure word for books)

Exception: in very formal or literary contexts, 这 can precede nouns directly.

◆ Atlas Note

这 and 那 together give you the fundamental ability to point at the world and talk about it. They are among the first words a child learns — and among the first a language learner needs.
