W0013 — 上

1 🔊

Pronunciation

上 Pinyin: shàng Tone: Fourth tone (falling) Meaning: above; on top of; to go up; to board; upper; preceding

2 📖

Core Usage

上 is primarily a directional and locational word. It works both as:

Location word (after a noun): on; above; on top of Verb: to go up; to board (a vehicle); to upload; to attend (class)

3 ✎

Examples — Location

书在桌子上。 Shū zài zhuōzi shàng. The book is on the table. Literal: Book / at / table / on.

墙上有一幅画。 Qiáng shàng yǒu yī fú huà. There is a painting on the wall. Literal: Wall / on / have / one / [measure word] / painting.

4 ✎

Examples — Verb

我们上课吧。 Wǒmen shàng kè ba. Let's start class. Literal: We / go-to / class / [suggestion particle].

他上车了。 Tā shàng chē le. He got on the vehicle. Literal: He / board / vehicle / [completed].

5 ❖

Compounds with 上

上面 — above; on top (more explicit than 上 alone) 上午 — morning (upper part of the day) 上课 — to attend class; class is in session 网上 — online (on the net) 以上 — above; more than; the above-mentioned

6 ❖

上 vs 下

上 — up; above; on 下 (xià) — down; below; under; to descend

楼上 — upstairs 楼下 — downstairs

7 ⚠

Common Mistake

上 as a location word always follows the noun it describes:

✅ 桌子上 (on the table) ❌ 上桌子 (means "to get onto the table" — a verb phrase)

◆ Atlas Note

上 is one of the most ancient characters in Chinese, originally an indicative sign — a mark above a line showing "higher than this." Its simplicity has made it one of the most productive characters in the language, appearing in hundreds of compounds related to upward movement, access, and precedence.
