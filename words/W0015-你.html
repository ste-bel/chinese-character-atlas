W0015 — 你

1 🔊

Pronunciation

你 Pinyin: nǐ Tone: Third tone (falling-rising) Meaning: you (singular, informal)

2 📖

Core Usage

你 is the ordinary second-person singular pronoun. It covers "you" as both subject and object.

Formal alternative: 您 (nín) — the polite form of you, used with elders, superiors, and in formal contexts. 您 is always singular.

3 ✎

Examples

你好！ Nǐ hǎo! Hello! (Lit: You good!)

你是哪里人？ Nǐ shì nǎlǐ rén? Where are you from? Literal: You / are / where / person?

我叫你了。 Wǒ jiào nǐ le. I called you. Literal: I / call / you / [completed].

4 ？

Question

你叫什么名字？ Nǐ jiào shénme míngzi? What is your name? Literal: You / called / what / name?

✅ 我叫… Wǒ jiào… My name is…

5 ❖

Plural

你们 (nǐmen) — you all; you (plural)

See W0016 — 们.

6 ❖

你 vs 您

你 is used with peers, friends, family members of similar age, and children. 您 is used with elders, people in authority, customers, and in formal speech.

Using 你 with someone who expects 您 can sound rude. Using 您 with a close friend can sound oddly formal or even ironic.

◆ Atlas Note

你好 (nǐ hǎo) — literally "you good" — is the most widely known Chinese phrase in the world, and the gateway through which most people first encounter Mandarin. Hidden inside this simple greeting is the Chinese logic: rather than "how are you?" (a question), Chinese traditionally offers a direct positive assertion about your state.
