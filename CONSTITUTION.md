# Chinese Character Atlas — Constitution

> **Every character tells a story. Every story opens a civilization.**

---

# Mission

The Chinese Character Atlas is an interactive encyclopedia dedicated to exploring the Chinese language through its words, characters, history, culture, and living usage.

This project is not a vocabulary list.

It is a long-term reference work that explains not only **what** Chinese means, but **why** it came to be.

---

# Vision

To become the most complete, interconnected, and historically accurate learning resource for Chinese, where every word is part of a larger web of language, culture, philosophy, and history.

---

# Core Principles

## 1. Accuracy Above All

- Never invent facts.
- Verify historical claims.
- Distinguish between fact, theory, and legend.
- Clearly state uncertainty when it exists.

---

## 2. Every Entry Has a Permanent Identity

Every page receives a stable identifier.

Examples:

- W0001 — Word
- CH0001 — Character
- CMP0001 — Component
- G0001 — Grammar
- B0001 — Book
- P0001 — Person
- I0001 — Idiom
- L0001 — Lesson

IDs never change.

---

## 3. No Unexplained Chinese

Every Chinese word appearing in an example must either:

- already have its own page, or
- appear in **New Words Introduced**.

Nothing is left unexplained.

---

## 4. Build Knowledge, Not Lists

Every entry should connect to related knowledge.

Words connect to:

- characters
- components
- grammar
- books
- idioms
- historical figures
- lessons
- pronunciation
- modern usage

---

## 5. Every Character Tells Its Story

Whenever possible explain:

- original drawing
- oracle bone form
- bronze inscription
- seal script
- regular script
- evolution of meaning
- evolution of pronunciation
- cultural significance

Do not reduce characters to radicals only.

---

## 6. Components Matter

Every important component receives its own page.

Examples:

- 日
- 女
- 木
- 心
- 口

Each component page links to every learned character containing it.

---

## 7. Words Are Not Characters

Characters and words are different concepts.

Example:

Character:

中

Word:

中国

Each has its own page.

---

## 8. Modern Chinese Matters

Every entry should explain:

- spoken vs written
- formal vs informal
- Mainland China
- Taiwan
- Hong Kong
- overseas Chinese
- internet language
- frequency
- current usage

---

## 9. Learning Should Be Beautiful

The Atlas should feel like:

- a museum
- a beautifully illustrated book
- a modern encyclopedia

Never like a dry dictionary.

---

## 10. Every Example Teaches

Every example should contain:

- Chinese
- pinyin
- natural English
- literal English
- audio

Questions also include:

- positive answer
- negative answer

---

## 11. Knowledge Grows Organically

The encyclopedia should become richer over time.

Instead of repeating information, entries link to each other.

Browsing is as important as searching.

---

## 12. Respect Chinese Civilization

The Atlas should present Chinese civilization with intellectual honesty and curiosity.

Historical context should be explained rather than judged.

The objective is understanding.
