CMP0007 — 言

1 ❖

Overview

Component: 言 Pinyin: yán Meaning: speech; words; to speak; language Category: Pictograph / Ideograph

2 ❖

Origin

言 is traditionally interpreted as a picture of a tongue (or sound waves) emerging from a mouth — showing the act of speaking. The top portion represents sounds or breath; the lower 口 (mouth) is the source.

3 ❖

As a Standalone Word

言 is used in formal, literary, and classical Chinese to mean word, speech, or to speak. In modern Mandarin it appears primarily in compounds:

语言 (yǔyán) — language 言语 (yányǔ) — speech; words 名言 (míngyán) — famous saying; quote 言论 (yánlùn) — speech; expression of opinion 言行 (yánxíng) — words and deeds

4 ⬡

As a Component (讠)

When 言 appears on the left side of a character, it takes a simplified compressed form: 讠(in Simplified Chinese). This is one of the most important components to recognise because it marks a large family of characters related to speaking, language, and communication.

Characters containing 讠/ 言:

Learned:

  • 说 (shuō) — to speak

Future:

  • 话 (huà) — speech; words; to speak
  • 语 (yǔ) — language; to speak
  • 请 (qǐng) — please; to invite
  • 谢 (xiè) — to thank
  • 读 (dú) — to read
  • 问 (wèn) — to ask
  • 让 (ràng) — to let; to allow
  • 认 (rèn) — to recognize
  • 记 (jì) — to record; to remember
  • 诉 (sù) — to tell; to sue
5 ❖

Traditional vs Simplified

In Traditional Chinese, 言 retains its full form as a left component (as in 說 — traditional for 说). In Simplified Chinese, it becomes 讠— two strokes instead of seven.

6 ⛰

Cultural Significance

言 carries weight in classical Chinese thought. Confucius was deeply concerned with the rectification of names (正名, zhèngmíng) — the idea that using words correctly is essential to moral and social order. 言 appears throughout classical texts as a marker of the seriousness of speech.

◆ Atlas Note

Recognising 讠(the simplified form of 言) on the left side of a character is one of the highest-yield pattern recognitions in learning Chinese characters. It immediately tells you: this character is likely related to speaking, language, or communication. Hundreds of common characters carry this component.
