CMP0003 — 口

1 ❖

Overview

Component: 口 Pinyin: kǒu Meaning: mouth; opening; entrance; classifier for people (in some dialects) and mouth-shaped objects Category: Pictograph

2 ❖

Origin

口 is a simple pictograph of an open mouth — a square representing the opening. The oracle bone forms were more rounded, but the shape settled into the clean square seen today.

3 ❖

As a Standalone Word

口 by itself means mouth, but in modern Mandarin it is more common in compounds:

出口 — exit; export (mouth going out) 入口 — entrance; import (mouth going in) 人口 — population (people-mouths) 口语 — spoken language 口音 — accent

4 ❖

Characters Using 口

The 口 component typically signals a connection to:

  • Speech and sound
  • Eating and the mouth
  • Openings and passages
  • Calling out

Characters containing 口:

  • 说 (shuō) — to speak (contains 口 via 言)
  • 吃 (chī) — to eat
  • 喝 (hē) — to drink
  • 叫 (jiào) — to call; to be named
  • 问 (wèn) — to ask
  • 唱 (chàng) — to sing
  • 吗 (ma) — question particle
  • 吧 (ba) — suggestion particle
  • 哦 (ō/ó) — oh!
  • 和 (hé) — and (contains 口)
  • 告 (gào) — to tell; to inform
5 ❖

Common Confusions

口 — mouth (open square) 日 — sun (square with a middle stroke) 目 — eye (tall rectangle with middle stroke)

口 is smaller and has no internal stroke. If there is a line inside, it is a different component.

◆ Atlas Note

口 appears in thousands of characters — particularly those related to speech, sound, and the acts of the mouth. When you see a square in a character and wonder what it means, 口 is often the answer. It is one of the most productive components in the entire writing system.
