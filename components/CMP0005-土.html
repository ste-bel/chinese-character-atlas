CMP0005 — 土

1 ❖

Overview

Component: 土 Pinyin: tǔ Meaning: earth; soil; ground; local; indigenous Category: Pictograph

2 ❖

Origin

土 is traditionally interpreted as showing a mound or clod of earth rising from the ground — a horizontal base line representing the ground, with a vertical stroke pushing upward through it, and a smaller horizontal stroke at the top representing the soil or mound itself. It is one of the Five Elements (五行) of classical Chinese cosmology: 金 (metal), 木 (wood), 水 (water), 火 (fire), and 土 (earth).

3 ❖

As a Standalone Word

土 means earth, soil, or ground and also carries the sense of local, native, or traditional (as opposed to foreign or modern):

土地 (tǔdì) — land; territory 泥土 (nítǔ) — mud; soil 土豆 (tǔdòu) — potato (earth bean) 本土 (běntǔ) — native; local; indigenous

4 ❖

Characters Using 土

土 appears in characters related to earth, ground, construction, and places:

Learned:

  • 在 (zài) — to be at; at (contains 土 as a component)

Future:

  • 地 (dì) — ground; earth; place
  • 城 (chéng) — city; wall
  • 坐 (zuò) — to sit
  • 块 (kuài) — piece; lump (measure word)
  • 墙 (qiáng) — wall
  • 场 (chǎng) — field; place
5 ❖

土 and the Five Elements

In traditional Chinese cosmology, 土 (earth) occupies the center position among the five elements — just as 中 (middle) does among directions. Earth is associated with:

  • The centre (中央)
  • Yellow (黄色)
  • The spleen
  • Late summer
  • The number five

This central position reflects 土's role as the foundation on which everything rests.

◆ Atlas Note

土 is radical #32 in the Kangxi system and one of the oldest concepts in Chinese civilization — a people deeply tied to agriculture and the land. The character appears in words about places, construction, and rootedness. To be 本土 (from the soil) is to be authentically of a place.
