CMP0002 — 人

1 ❖

Overview

Component: 人 Pinyin: rén Meaning: person; human being Category: Pictograph

2 ❖

Origin

人 is one of the most ancient and recognisable pictographs in Chinese. The oracle bone form shows a simple side view of a human figure — a person with a head, leaning torso, and legs. Over millennia, the figure became more abstract, but the essential shape of a person in profile remains visible in the modern character.

3 ⬡

Form When Used as a Component

When 人 appears on the left side of a character, it typically takes a compressed form called 亻(the "person radical"). This modified form appears in:

他 (tā) — he 你 (nǐ) — you 们 (men) — plural suffix 做 (zuò) — to do

When 人 appears elsewhere (bottom, top), it keeps its full form:

大 (dà) — big (person with arms spread) 天 (tiān) — sky (above a person)

4 ❖

Characters Using 人 / 亻

Learned:

  • 人 — person
  • 他 — he
  • 你 — you
  • 们 — plural suffix

Future:

  • 做 — to do
  • 什 — (in 什么, what)
  • 们 — plural suffix
  • 但 — but; however
  • 住 — to live; to stay
  • 信 — letter; to believe
  • 休 — to rest
  • 仁 — benevolence (Confucian virtue)
5 ▦

Meaning Pattern

Characters containing 亻 frequently relate to people, human activities, human qualities, or roles:

  • Actions people perform
  • Relationships between people
  • Human traits and virtues
6 ⛰

Cultural Significance

人 occupies a central place in Chinese thought. Confucian philosophy is built around the relationship between 人 and virtue, society, and Heaven. The concept of 仁 (rén — benevolence, humaneness) shares its sound with 人 and is considered by Confucius to be the highest human virtue.

7 ❖

Common Confusions

人 (rén) — person 入 (rù) — to enter (similar shape but the strokes differ)

人 has a longer left stroke that sweeps outward. 入 has a shorter left stroke that points inward.

◆ Atlas Note

人 is Radical #9 in the Kangxi radical system and appears in hundreds of characters. Recognising 人 and its compressed form 亻 unlocks a large portion of the Chinese character system at once.
