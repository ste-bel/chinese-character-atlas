CH0001 — 是

Overview

Character: 是

Pinyin: shì

Traditional: 是

Unicode: U+662F

Stroke Count: 9

Meaning

Today, 是 is most commonly associated with the verb "to be" in Modern Mandarin.

Historically, however, the character existed long before Modern Mandarin and originally conveyed the idea of correctness, rightness, or "this is so."

Character Construction

Top component:

日 (sun)

Lower component:

A historical form that has changed over thousands of years.

Modern learners often notice 日, but the complete character should not be interpreted as simply "sun + something."

Historical Evolution

Oracle Bone Script

No confirmed oracle-bone form is known.

Bronze Script

Early forms begin appearing during the Bronze inscription period.

Seal Script

The character becomes much closer to the modern form.

Clerical Script

The shape becomes flatter and wider.

Regular Script

The modern form 是 is standardized.

Etymology

Traditional Chinese dictionaries explain 是 as relating to correctness and affirmation.

Modern historical linguistics considers its evolution more complex and does not reduce it to a simple combination of radicals.

Pronunciation

Modern Mandarin:

shì

Fourth tone.

Traditional Form

Traditional Chinese uses exactly the same character:

是
