# CH0002 — 的

## Overview

**Character:** 的
**Pinyin:** de (particle) / dí (truly, indeed — literary) / dì (target, goal)
**Stroke Count:** 8
**Unicode:** U+7684
**Traditional:** 的 (unchanged)

## The Most Frequent Character in Modern Mandarin

的 is the single most common character in modern written Chinese. In most large corpora, it appears more frequently than any other character. Despite this, its grammatical function — not its pictorial origin — is what matters for learners.

## Construction

的 is composed of:

- 白 (bái) — white; bright — on the left
- 勺 (sháo) — ladle; spoon — on the right

This combination is purely historical. The original character had a different meaning (bright; target). Its current use as a grammatical particle is a phonetic borrowing — the character was repurposed for a word that sounded like it.

## Historical Role

In classical Chinese, 之 (zhī) performed a similar possessive function. In modern Mandarin, 的 has largely replaced 之 in spoken and informal written language. 之 survives in formal, literary, and idiomatic contexts.

## Pronunciation Notes

的 is nearly always read as de (neutral tone) when used as the attributive particle — the most common function. It loses all stress in connected speech.

的确 (díquè) — indeed; certainly — is an exception where 的 is read dí.

目的 (mùdì) — goal; aim; purpose — is where 的 reads dì.
