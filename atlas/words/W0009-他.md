# W0009 — 他

## Pronunciation

**他**
**Pinyin:** tā
**Tone:** First tone (high and level)
**Meaning:** he; him; his (informal)

## The Third-Person Pronouns

Mandarin has three written third-person pronouns that all sound identical:

| Written | Used for | Pinyin |
|---------|----------|--------|
| 他      | males, general | tā |
| 她      | females | tā |
| 它      | animals, objects | tā |

In spoken Mandarin, there is no distinction at all. The difference exists only in writing.

## Core Usage

他 works as both subject ("he") and object ("him"). No case distinction.

## Examples

{{audio:他是我的朋友。}}
他是我的朋友。
*Tā shì wǒ de péngyou.*
He is my friend.
Literal: He / is / my / friend.

{{audio:我不认识他。}}
我不认识他。
*Wǒ bù rènshi tā.*
I don't know him.
Literal: I / not / know / him.

{{audio:他在哪里？}}
他在哪里？
*Tā zài nǎlǐ?*
Where is he?

## Plural

他们 (tāmen) — they; them (male or mixed group)
她们 (tāmen) — they; them (female group only)

See [[W0016|W0016 — 们]].

## Common Mistake

When speaking, there is no way to distinguish 他, 她, and 它. Context carries the meaning. Do not struggle to "pronounce the gender" — it does not exist in sound.

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
The character 她 (she) was invented in the early 20th century as Chinese modernised and translators needed a way to render the gendered English "she" in written form. Before that, 他 served for everyone. The spoken language never changed — only the written form added a gender distinction.
{{/atlas-note}}
