# W0007 — 在

## Pronunciation

**在**
**Pinyin:** zài
**Tone:** Fourth tone (falling)
**Meaning:** at; in; on; to be at/in; to exist somewhere

## Core Usage

在 has two related functions:

**Function 1 — Verb: to be (somewhere)**
Subject + 在 + Location

**Function 2 — Preposition: at/in/on**
Subject + 在 + Location + Verb

When 在 comes before the verb, it tells you *where* the action happens.
When 在 is the main verb, it tells you *where* something is.

## Examples — Verb

{{audio:她在家。}}
她在家。
*Tā zài jiā.*
She is at home.
Literal: She / at / home.

{{audio:书在桌子上。}}
书在桌子上。
*Shū zài zhuōzi shàng.*
The book is on the table.
Literal: Book / at / table / on.

## Examples — Preposition

{{audio:我在家工作。}}
我在家工作。
*Wǒ zài jiā gōngzuò.*
I work at home.
Literal: I / at / home / work.

{{audio:他在学校学习。}}
他在学校学习。
*Tā zài xuéxiào xuéxí.*
He studies at school.
Literal: He / at / school / study.

## Question

{{audio:你在哪里？}}
你在哪里？
*Nǐ zài nǎlǐ?*
Where are you?
Literal: You / at / where?

✅ {{audio:我在家。}}
我在家。
*Wǒ zài jiā.*
I'm at home.

✅ {{audio:我在公司。}}
我在公司。
*Wǒ zài gōngsī.*
I'm at the office.

## 在 vs 是

在 locates — it tells you *where* something is.
是 identifies — it tells you *what* something is.

✅ 他在北京 — He is in Beijing (location)
✅ 他是加拿大人 — He is Canadian (identity)
❌ 他是在北京 (incorrect mix)

## Common Mistake

Location words in Chinese often follow their noun:

书在桌子**上** — book on the table
人在楼上 — person upstairs

The directional word (上, 下, 里, 外) comes *after* the location noun, not before.

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
在 does double duty as both a main verb and a preposition — two jobs that English splits between "to be" and "at/in/on." Noticing whether 在 comes before or after the main verb will tell you which function it is serving.
{{/atlas-note}}
