# W0003 — 我

## Pronunciation

**我**
**Pinyin:** wǒ
**Tone:** Third tone (falling-rising)
**Meaning:** I; me; my (informal)

## Core Usage

我 is the first person singular pronoun. It does not change form for subject or object — the same word works for "I" and "me."

我 + verb → I [do something]
verb + 我 → [someone does something] to me

## Examples

{{audio:我是学生。}}
我是学生。
*Wǒ shì xuésheng.*
I am a student.
Literal: I / am / student.

{{audio:他叫我。}}
他叫我。
*Tā jiào wǒ.*
He is calling me.
Literal: He / calls / me.

{{audio:这是我的。}}
这是我的。
*Zhè shì wǒ de.*
This is mine.
Literal: This / is / mine.

## Question

{{audio:你叫我吗？}}
你叫我吗？
*Nǐ jiào wǒ ma?*
Are you calling me?

✅ {{audio:是的，我叫你。}}
是的，我叫你。
*Shì de, wǒ jiào nǐ.*
Yes, I'm calling you.

❌ {{audio:不，我没叫你。}}
不，我没叫你。
*Bù, wǒ méi jiào nǐ.*
No, I wasn't calling you.

## Plural

我们 (wǒmen) — we; us

Adding 们 makes 我 plural. See [[W0016|W0016 — 们]].

## Common Mistake

English speakers sometimes omit 我 as the subject — Chinese does allow topic-drop in context, but beginners should keep 我 explicit until the pattern becomes natural.

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
Chinese has no distinction between "I" and "me." The same 我 serves both functions. No case endings, no conjugation — just 我, everywhere you need it.
{{/atlas-note}}
