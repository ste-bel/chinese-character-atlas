# W0012 — 来

## Pronunciation

**来**
**Pinyin:** lái
**Tone:** Second tone (rising)
**Meaning:** to come; to arrive; (used to indicate an action is about to happen)

## Core Usage

来 indicates movement toward the speaker or toward a reference point. It contrasts with 去 (qù — to go), which indicates movement away.

来 also serves as a directional complement after verbs of motion, and colloquially can signal that someone is about to do something.

## Examples

{{audio:你来这里。}}
你来这里。
*Nǐ lái zhèlǐ.*
Come here.
Literal: You / come / here.

{{audio:他来了。}}
他来了。
*Tā lái le.*
He has arrived. / He's here.
Literal: He / come / [completed/change of state].

{{audio:我来中国学习。}}
我来中国学习。
*Wǒ lái Zhōngguó xuéxí.*
I came to China to study.
Literal: I / come / China / study.

## Question

{{audio:你来不来？}}
你来不来？
*Nǐ lái bu lái?*
Are you coming or not?

✅ {{audio:我来。}}
我来。
*Wǒ lái.*
I'm coming.

❌ {{audio:我不来。}}
我不来。
*Wǒ bù lái.*
I'm not coming.

## 来 vs 去

来 — toward speaker / toward destination (from speaker's view)
去 — away from speaker / toward destination (from speaker's view)

我来 — I'm coming (toward you)
我去 — I'm going (away from here)

## Colloquial Use

来 often signals eagerness or readiness to act:

我来做！ — Let me do it! / I'll do it!
你来 — Your turn. / Go ahead.
