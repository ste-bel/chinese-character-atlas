# W0004 — 不

## Pronunciation

**不**
**Pinyin:** bù (4th tone before most syllables)
**Tone change:** bú (2nd tone) before another 4th-tone syllable

不 changes tone automatically before a 4th-tone syllable. This is one of the few mandatory tone sandhi rules beginners must learn.

不好 → bù hǎo (before 3rd tone — stays 4th)
不是 → bú shì (before 4th tone — becomes 2nd)

## Core Usage

不 negates verbs and adjectives.

不 + verb → does not [verb]
不 + adjective → not [adjective]

## Examples

{{audio:我不是学生。}}
我不是学生。
*Wǒ bú shì xuésheng.*
I am not a student.
Literal: I / not / am / student.

{{audio:他不来。}}
他不来。
*Tā bù lái.*
He is not coming.
Literal: He / not / come.

{{audio:这个不好。}}
这个不好。
*Zhège bù hǎo.*
This one is not good.
Literal: This [one] / not / good.

## Question

{{audio:你去不去？}}
你去不去？
*Nǐ qù bu qù?*
Are you going or not?

This A-不-A question pattern is one of the most natural ways to ask a yes/no question in Mandarin.

✅ {{audio:我去。}}
我去。
*Wǒ qù.*
I'm going.

❌ {{audio:我不去。}}
我不去。
*Wǒ bù qù.*
I'm not going.

## 不 vs 没

不 negates habitual or intentional action, and negates 是 and adjectives.
没 (méi) negates 有 (to have) and past completed actions.

❌ 我不吃了 (sounds unnatural for "I didn't eat")
✅ 我没吃 (I didn't eat — use 没 for past events)

## Common Mistake

English speakers say "不" for "no" in all contexts. In Mandarin, the natural standalone "no" in response to a question is often just repeating the negated verb:

Q: 你是学生吗？
A: 不是。(Not: just 不)
