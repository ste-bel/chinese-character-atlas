# W0018 — 大

## Pronunciation

**大**
**Pinyin:** dà (occasionally tài in certain compounds)
**Tone:** Fourth tone (falling)
**Meaning:** big; large; great; adult; eldest; greatly; much

## Core Usage

大 is a common adjective that also forms part of many important compound words. It contrasts with 小 (xiǎo — small, little, young).

## Examples

{{audio:这个很大。}}
这个很大。
*Zhège hěn dà.*
This one is very big.
Literal: This / [measure word] / very / big.

{{audio:他是我的大哥。}}
他是我的大哥。
*Tā shì wǒ de dàgē.*
He is my eldest brother.
Literal: He / is / my / eldest / brother.

{{audio:北京是一个很大的城市。}}
北京是一个很大的城市。
*Běijīng shì yīgè hěn dà de chéngshì.*
Beijing is a very large city.

## Question

{{audio:你的房间大不大？}}
你的房间大不大？
*Nǐ de fángjiān dà bú dà?*
Is your room big?
(A-not-A question pattern)

✅ {{audio:挺大的。}}
挺大的。
*Tǐng dà de.*
Quite big.

❌ {{audio:不大。}}
不大。
*Bú dà.*
Not very big.

## Common Compounds

大学 — university (great learning)
大人 — adult (big person)
大家 — everyone (big family)
大概 — probably; approximately
大学生 — university student
很大 — very big
最大 — biggest; largest
长大 — to grow up

## 大 as "Greatly / Very" (before verbs)

大笑 — to laugh heartily (greatly laugh)
大哭 — to cry a lot (greatly cry)
大吃一惊 — to be greatly shocked

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
大 is a pictograph of a person standing with arms fully outstretched — showing maximum extent. This image of a person spreading wide to show size is one of the most intuitive origins in the Chinese writing system. From this simple picture came not only "big" and "large," but also concepts of importance, seniority, and greatness.
{{/atlas-note}}
