# W0002 — 的

## Pronunciation

**的**
**Pinyin:** de (neutral tone — unstressed)
**Meaning:** possessive particle; attributive particle

的 is almost always unstressed. It is one of the most common syllables in all of Mandarin.

## Core Usage

的 connects a modifier to a noun. It works like "'s" in English — or like the word "of" — but also appears after adjectives before nouns.

**Pattern 1 — Possession:**

A 的 + Noun
A's Noun

**Pattern 2 — Description:**

Adjective + 的 + Noun
A [adjective] noun

## Examples

{{audio:这是我的书。}}
这是我的书。
*Zhè shì wǒ de shū.*
This is my book.
Literal: This / is / I's / book.

{{audio:她是我的老师。}}
她是我的老师。
*Tā shì wǒ de lǎoshī.*
She is my teacher.
Literal: She / is / I's / teacher.

{{audio:这是一本很好的书。}}
这是一本很好的书。
*Zhè shì yī běn hěn hǎo de shū.*
This is a very good book.
Literal: This / is / one / [measure word] / very / good / 的 / book.

## Question

{{audio:这是你的吗？}}
这是你的吗？
*Zhè shì nǐ de ma?*
Is this yours?

✅ {{audio:是的，是我的。}}
是的，是我的。
*Shì de, shì wǒ de.*
Yes, it's mine.

❌ {{audio:不，不是我的。}}
不，不是我的。
*Bù, bú shì wǒ de.*
No, it isn't mine.

## When 的 Can Be Dropped

的 is often omitted between pronouns and close relationships:

我妈妈 (not 我的妈妈) — my mother
你朋友 (not 你的朋友) — your friend (informal)

Adding 的 is not wrong — dropping it sounds more natural in close relationships.

## Common Mistake

❌ 的 does not mean "yes." 是的 means "yes" only as a fixed phrase.

❌ Treating 的 like an English apostrophe-s: 的 comes before the noun, not after the possessor.

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
的 is the most frequent character in modern Mandarin. In any long Chinese text you will see it appear more often than any other character. Master the feeling of 的 early — it will follow you everywhere.
