# W0015 — 你

## Pronunciation

**你**
**Pinyin:** nǐ
**Tone:** Third tone (falling-rising)
**Meaning:** you (singular, informal)

## Core Usage

你 is the ordinary second-person singular pronoun. It covers "you" as both subject and object.

**Formal alternative:** 您 (nín) — the polite form of you, used with elders, superiors, and in formal contexts. 您 is always singular.

## Examples

{{audio:你好！}}
你好！
*Nǐ hǎo!*
Hello! (Lit: You good!)

{{audio:你是哪里人？}}
你是哪里人？
*Nǐ shì nǎlǐ rén?*
Where are you from?
Literal: You / are / where / person?

{{audio:我叫你了。}}
我叫你了。
*Wǒ jiào nǐ le.*
I called you.
Literal: I / call / you / [completed].

## Plural

你们 (nǐmen) — you all; you (plural)

See [[W0016|W0016 — 们]].

## 你 vs 您

你 is used with peers, friends, family members of similar age, and children.
您 is used with elders, people in authority, customers, and in formal speech.

Using 你 with someone who expects 您 can sound rude.
Using 您 with a close friend can sound oddly formal or even ironic.

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
你好 (nǐ hǎo) — literally "you good" — is the most widely known Chinese phrase in the world, and the gateway through which most people first encounter Mandarin. Hidden inside this simple greeting is the Chinese logic: rather than "how are you?" (a question), Chinese traditionally offers a direct positive assertion about your state.
{{/atlas-note}}
