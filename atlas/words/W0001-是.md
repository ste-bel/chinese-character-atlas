# W0001 — 是

## Pronunciation

**是**
**Pinyin:** shì
**Tone:** Fourth tone (falling)
**Meaning:** to be; is; are; am; yes; correct; indeed

## Core Usage

是 identifies what something is. It connects a subject to a noun or noun phrase.

Pattern:

A 是 B
A is B

是 is not used before adjectives. Adjectives in Chinese act as their own predicates.

## Examples

{{audio:我是加拿大人。}}
我是加拿大人。
*Wǒ shì Jiānádà rén.*
I am Canadian.
Literal: I / am / Canada / person.

{{audio:她是我的老师。}}
她是我的老师。
*Tā shì wǒ de lǎoshī.*
She is my teacher.
Literal: She / is / my / teacher.

{{audio:这是什么？}}
这是什么？
*Zhè shì shénme?*
What is this?
Literal: This / is / what?

## Question

{{audio:你是学生吗？}}
你是学生吗？
*Nǐ shì xuésheng ma?*
Are you a student?

✅ {{audio:是的，我是学生。}}
是的，我是学生。
*Shì de, wǒ shì xuésheng.*
Yes, I am a student.

❌ {{audio:不是，我不是学生。}}
不是，我不是学生。
*Bú shì, wǒ bú shì xuésheng.*
No, I am not a student.

## 是 vs Adjectives

是 cannot connect a subject to an adjective directly. Adjectives stand alone as predicates, usually preceded by 很 (hěn — very) in neutral statements.

✅ {{audio:我很好。}}
我很好。
*Wǒ hěn hǎo.*
I am good. (Lit: I very good.)

❌ 我是好。
Unnatural. Do not use 是 before an adjective.

## 是 vs 有 vs 在

Three verbs that English collapses into "to be":

是 — identity: A is B (A = B)
有 — possession/existence: A has B; there is B
在 — location: A is at/in/on [place]

✅ 他是老师 — He is a teacher (identity)
✅ 他有时间 — He has time (possession)
✅ 他在家 — He is at home (location)

## Common Compounds

是的 — yes (confirmation)
不是 — is not; no
就是 — exactly; precisely; it is just that
可是 — but; however
于是 — therefore; as a result
还是 — still; or (in questions)

## Common Mistake

English speakers use 是 before adjectives. Chinese does not.

❌ 我是高兴 (wrong)
✅ 我很高兴 (I am happy)
✅ 我高兴 (I am happy — adjective as predicate, more emphatic)

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
English uses "to be" everywhere — for identity, location, existence, description, and more. Chinese splits these functions across 是, 有, and 在. Mastering which verb to use in which situation is one of the first and most important distinctions a learner must make.
{{/atlas-note}}
