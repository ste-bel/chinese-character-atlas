# W0006 — 人

## Pronunciation

**人**
**Pinyin:** rén
**Tone:** Second tone (rising)
**Meaning:** person; people; human being

## Core Usage

人 is both singular and plural in Chinese. Context determines number.

一个人 — one person
很多人 — many people / a lot of people
中国人 — Chinese person/people

人 also combines with other words to form words about types of people:

商人 — merchant
工人 — worker
外国人 — foreigner

## Examples

{{audio:她是一个好人。}}
她是一个好人。
*Tā shì yīgè hǎo rén.*
She is a good person.
Literal: She / is / one / [measure word] / good / person.

{{audio:这里有很多人。}}
这里有很多人。
*Zhèlǐ yǒu hěnduō rén.*
There are many people here.
Literal: Here / have / very many / people.

{{audio:你是哪国人？}}
你是哪国人？
*Nǐ shì nǎ guó rén?*
What country are you from?
Literal: You / are / which / country / person?

## Question

{{audio:你是中国人吗？}}
你是中国人吗？
*Nǐ shì Zhōngguó rén ma?*
Are you Chinese?

✅ {{audio:是的，我是中国人。}}
是的，我是中国人。
*Shì de, wǒ shì Zhōngguó rén.*
Yes, I am Chinese.

❌ {{audio:不，我不是中国人。}}
不，我不是中国人。
*Bù, wǒ bú shì Zhōngguó rén.*
No, I'm not Chinese.

## Common Mistake

人 does not require a measure word when used generically. Use 个 when counting specific people:

✅ 人很多 (People are many / There are many people)
✅ 三个人 (three people)
❌ 三人 (archaic or formal register only)

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
人 is one of the oldest and most pictographic characters in Chinese. The original oracle bone form shows a person bending forward — a simple side view of a human figure. Nearly every Chinese concept about humanity, society, and relationships includes 人.
{{/atlas-note}}
