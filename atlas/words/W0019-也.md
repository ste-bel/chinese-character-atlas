# W0019 — 也

## Pronunciation

**也**
**Pinyin:** yě
**Tone:** Third tone (falling-rising)
**Meaning:** also; too; as well; either (in negatives); even

## Core Usage

也 is an adverb. It must appear directly before the verb or adjective it modifies — unlike English "too" or "also," which can appear at the end of a sentence.

Subject + 也 + Verb/Adjective

## Examples

{{audio:我也是学生。}}
我也是学生。
*Wǒ yě shì xuésheng.*
I am also a student.
Literal: I / also / am / student.

{{audio:她也来了。}}
她也来了。
*Tā yě lái le.*
She came too.
Literal: She / also / came / [completed].

{{audio:这个也不错。}}
这个也不错。
*Zhège yě búcuò.*
This one is also pretty good.

## Question

{{audio:你也是加拿大人吗？}}
你也是加拿大人吗？
*Nǐ yě shì Jiānádà rén ma?*
Are you also Canadian?

✅ {{audio:是的，我也是。}}
是的，我也是。
*Shì de, wǒ yě shì.*
Yes, I am too.

❌ {{audio:不，我不是。}}
不，我不是。
*Bù, wǒ bú shì.*
No, I'm not.

## 也 in Negative Sentences

In negative sentences, 也 means "either" or "not…either":

{{audio:我也不知道。}}
我也不知道。
*Wǒ yě bù zhīdào.*
I don't know either.
Literal: I / also / not / know.

## Common Mistake

❌ 我是学生也 (wrong — 也 must come before the verb, not after)
✅ 我也是学生 (correct)

The position of 也 is fixed: it must precede the predicate.

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
也 has an ancient and somewhat mysterious origin. Early forms resemble a snake or a figure with extended lines — its connection to the meaning "also" is no longer transparent from its shape. Linguists believe it was originally a phonetic loan character: borrowed for its sound, not its picture. Today it is entirely grammatical — one of those characters whose original meaning has been completely replaced by usage.
{{/atlas-note}}
