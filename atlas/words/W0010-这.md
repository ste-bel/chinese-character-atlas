# W0010 — 这

## Pronunciation

**这**
**Pinyin:** zhè (or zhèi before measure words)
**Tone:** Fourth tone (falling)
**Meaning:** this; these

Before a measure word, 这 is often pronounced zhèi in natural speech (though zhè is always correct in writing):

这个 → zhège or zhèige

## Core Usage

这 points to something near the speaker. It pairs with 那 (nà — that), which points to something farther away.

这 + Measure Word + Noun → this [noun]
这 + 是 → this is...
这里 / 这儿 → here

## Examples

{{audio:这是什么？}}
这是什么？
*Zhè shì shénme?*
What is this?
Literal: This / is / what?

{{audio:这个很好。}}
这个很好。
*Zhège hěn hǎo.*
This one is very good.
Literal: This / [measure word] / very / good.

{{audio:这里有人吗？}}
这里有人吗？
*Zhèlǐ yǒu rén ma?*
Is anyone here?
Literal: Here / have / person?

## Question

{{audio:这是你的吗？}}
这是你的吗？
*Zhè shì nǐ de ma?*
Is this yours?

✅ {{audio:是的，这是我的。}}
是的，这是我的。
*Shì de, zhè shì wǒ de.*
Yes, this is mine.

❌ {{audio:不，这不是我的。}}
不，这不是我的。
*Bù, zhè bú shì wǒ de.*
No, this isn't mine.

## 这 vs 那

这 — this (near speaker)
那 (nà) — that (away from speaker)

这个人 — this person (here)
那个人 — that person (there)

## Common Mistake

这 cannot stand alone before a noun in most contexts — a measure word is usually required:

❌ 这书 (unnatural in standard speech)
✅ 这本书 (this book — 本 is the measure word for books)

Exception: in very formal or literary contexts, 这 can precede nouns directly.

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
这 and 那 together give you the fundamental ability to point at the world and talk about it. They are among the first words a child learns — and among the first a language learner needs.
