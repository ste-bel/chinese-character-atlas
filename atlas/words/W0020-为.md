# W0020 — 为

## Pronunciation

为 has two pronunciations with different grammatical roles:

**wéi** (2nd tone) — verb: to be; to act as; to serve as
**wèi** (4th tone) — preposition: for; for the sake of; because of

**Traditional form:** 為

## Core Usage as wéi (verb)

成为 → to become
认为 → to think; to consider (lit: to recognize as)
以为 → to mistakenly think; to assume

## Core Usage as wèi (preposition)

为了 (wèile) → for the purpose of; in order to
为什么 (wèishénme) → why? (for what reason?)

## Examples

{{audio:为什么？}}
为什么？
*Wèishénme?*
Why?
Literal: For / what / reason?

{{audio:他成为了一名老师。}}
他成为了一名老师。
*Tā chéngwéi le yī míng lǎoshī.*
He became a teacher.
Literal: He / became / [completed] / one / [measure word] / teacher.

{{audio:我为你感到骄傲。}}
我为你感到骄傲。
*Wǒ wèi nǐ gǎndào jiāo'ào.*
I am proud of you.
Literal: I / for / you / feel / proud.

{{audio:你为什么学中文？}}
你为什么学中文？
*Nǐ wèishénme xué Zhōngwén?*
Why are you learning Chinese?

✅ {{audio:因为中文很有意思。}}
因为中文很有意思。
*Yīnwèi Zhōngwén hěn yǒu yìsi.*
Because Chinese is very interesting.

## Key Compound: 为什么

为什么 (wèishénme) — why — is one of the first question words a learner needs. It literally means "for what" or "for what reason."

## Key Compound: 因为

因为 (yīnwèi) — because — pairs naturally with 所以 (suǒyǐ — therefore) in complex sentences:

因为我喜欢中文，所以我来学习。
Because I like Chinese, I came to study it.

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
The traditional character 為 showed a hand leading an elephant — representing effort, work, and doing things for a purpose. The simplified 为 retains the same sound and meaning but reduces the visual complexity significantly. Why appears in some of the most common question-and-answer structures in Mandarin, making it an essential word despite its HSK 2 designation.
{{/atlas-note}}
