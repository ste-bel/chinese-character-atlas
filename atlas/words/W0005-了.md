# W0005 — 了

## Pronunciation

了 has two pronunciations with distinct meanings:

**le** (neutral tone) — grammatical particle: completion, change of state
**liǎo** (3rd tone) — verb: to finish; to understand; to end

This page covers **le** — the particle. It is by far the more common usage.

## Core Usage

了 (le) has two main grammatical functions:

**Function 1 — Completion aspect (verb suffix):**
Placed directly after a verb to indicate the action was completed.

Verb + 了 → [completed action]

**Function 2 — Change of state (sentence-final):**
Placed at the end of a sentence to indicate a new situation has arisen.

Sentence + 了 → [new situation / changed state]

These two uses are distinct, though they sometimes overlap.

## Examples — Completion

{{audio:我吃了。}}
我吃了。
*Wǒ chī le.*
I've eaten. / I ate.
Literal: I / eat / [completed].

{{audio:他走了。}}
他走了。
*Tā zǒu le.*
He left.
Literal: He / leave / [completed].

{{audio:我买了一本书。}}
我买了一本书。
*Wǒ mǎi le yī běn shū.*
I bought a book.
Literal: I / buy / [completed] / one / [measure word] / book.

## Examples — Change of State

{{audio:天黑了。}}
天黑了。
*Tiān hēi le.*
It's gotten dark.
Literal: Sky / dark / [new situation].

{{audio:我懂了。}}
我懂了。
*Wǒ dǒng le.*
I understand now. (I didn't before.)
Literal: I / understand / [change of state].

## Common Mistake

了 does not simply mean "past tense." Mandarin has no tense — only aspect. 了 indicates completion or change, not that something happened in the past.

❌ 昨天我是去了商店 (unnatural over-use)
✅ 昨天我去了商店 (I went to the store yesterday)

Also: negation with 没 removes 了 entirely:
✅ 我没吃 (not: 我没吃了)

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
了 is one of the most discussed particles in Chinese grammar — and one of the most misunderstood. Students try to treat it as a past tense marker. It is not. It marks completion and change. A situation can be completed in the future: 明天我吃了再走 (Tomorrow I'll leave after eating).
{{/atlas-note}}
