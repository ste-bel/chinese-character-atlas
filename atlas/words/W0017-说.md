# W0017 — 说

## Pronunciation

**说**
**Pinyin:** shuō
**Tone:** First tone (high and level)
**Meaning:** to say; to speak; to tell; to explain; speech

**Traditional form:** 說

The initial consonant 'sh' is retroflex — the tongue curls back. This is different from the English 'sh' in "she."

## Core Usage

说 is the primary verb for speaking and saying in Mandarin. It is used when quoting speech, reporting what someone said, and discussing language ability.

说 + Language → to speak [language]
说 + Quoted content → to say [something]
说明 → to explain; explanation

## Examples

{{audio:她说中文说得很好。}}
她说中文说得很好。
*Tā shuō Zhōngwén shuō de hěn hǎo.*
She speaks Chinese very well.
Literal: She / speak / Chinese / speak / [manner complement] / very / well.

{{audio:他说他不来了。}}
他说他不来了。
*Tā shuō tā bù lái le.*
He said he's not coming.
Literal: He / said / he / not / come / [change of state].

{{audio:你在说什么？}}
你在说什么？
*Nǐ zài shuō shénme?*
What are you saying?
Literal: You / in the process of / say / what?

## Question

{{audio:你会说中文吗？}}
你会说中文吗？
*Nǐ huì shuō Zhōngwén ma?*
Can you speak Chinese?

✅ {{audio:会说一点儿。}}
会说一点儿。
*Huì shuō yīdiǎnr.*
I can speak a little.

❌ {{audio:不会说。}}
不会说。
*Bù huì shuō.*
I can't speak it.

## Common Compounds

说话 — to speak; to talk (general)
说明 — to explain; explanation; instructions
小说 — novel (fiction)
传说 — legend; folklore
听说 — to hear (that); it is said that

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
说 contains 言 (speech/words) as its semantic component — the part that carries meaning — and 兑 as its phonetic component, which historically provided sound guidance. This is the logic of the vast majority of Chinese characters: one part suggests meaning, one part suggests sound. 说 is a perfect example of this phono-semantic structure.
{{/atlas-note}}
