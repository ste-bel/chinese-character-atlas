# CMP0004 — 月

## Overview

**Component:** 月
**Pinyin:** yuè
**Meaning:** moon; month
**Category:** Pictograph

## Origin

月 began as a pictograph of the crescent moon — a curved form with a mark inside. Over time it flattened into the rectangular shape used today. The original curvature of the moon is still suggested by the shorter stroke inside.

## As a Standalone Word

月 means both moon and month — the lunar calendar made these the same concept. Modern compounds include:

月亮 (yuèliang) — the moon (common spoken word)
月份 (yuèfèn) — month (as a time unit)
月球 (yuèqiú) — the moon (scientific term)

Months of the year are expressed simply as number + 月:

一月 — January
二月 — February
十二月 — December

## Characters Using 月

月 appears in characters related to time, light, and the body:

**Learned:**
- 有 (yǒu) — to have (月 + 又)

**Future:**
- 明 (míng) — bright (日 + 月)
- 期 (qī) — period of time
- 朝 (zhāo/cháo) — morning; dynasty
- 望 (wàng) — to hope; full moon

## 月 vs 肉

A frequent source of confusion: when 月 appears as a left-side component in many characters, it is actually 肉 (ròu — flesh, meat) that has been graphically merged with 月 over centuries. Characters with body part meanings (脸 face, 胳 arm, 腿 leg, 脚 foot) contain 肉, not 月, despite looking identical in print.

月 → time, light, celestial
肉/月 → body parts, flesh

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
The moon's role in the Chinese calendar runs deep. For most of Chinese history, the calendar was lunar. Festivals like the Mid-Autumn Festival (中秋节) and the Spring Festival are still calculated by the lunar calendar. 月 carries all of this cultural weight in two strokes.
{{/atlas-note}}
