# CMP0006 — 大

## Overview

**Component:** 大
**Pinyin:** dà
**Meaning:** big; great; a person with arms spread wide
**Category:** Pictograph

## Origin

大 is a pictograph of a person standing upright with arms extended to their full width — showing maximum extent, bigness, greatness. The oracle bone form makes the human figure unmistakable: a head at the top, outstretched arms to the sides, and legs below.

This is the same person seen in 人 (rén — person), but with arms spread to indicate size.

## Relationship to 人 and 天

The three characters 人, 大, and 天 share a family of human-figure shapes:

人 (rén) — person: arms hanging naturally
大 (dà) — big: arms spread wide
天 (tiān) — sky/heaven: a horizontal line above the person (what is above people)

## Characters Using 大

**Learned:**
- 大 — big; great

**Future:**
- 天 (tiān) — sky; heaven; day (大 + top stroke)
- 太 (tài) — too; excessively (大 + dot)
- 夫 (fū) — husband; man (大 + top stroke variant)
- 头 (tóu) — head
- 夺 (duó) — to seize

## 大 as a Semantic Component

When 大 appears inside other characters, it often contributes a sense of largeness, greatness, or extension — though this is not always transparent in modern forms.

## Atlas Note

{{atlas-note}}
The journey from a person standing with arms out to the abstract concept of "big" is one of the most intuitive leaps in the Chinese writing system. 大 captures the simple gesture of showing size — the same gesture a child uses anywhere in the world when asked "how big?"
{{/atlas-note}}
