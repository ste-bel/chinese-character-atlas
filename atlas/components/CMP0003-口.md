# CMP0003 — 口

## Overview

**Component:** 口
**Pinyin:** kǒu
**Meaning:** mouth; opening; entrance; classifier for people (in some dialects) and mouth-shaped objects
**Category:** Pictograph

## Origin

口 is a simple pictograph of an open mouth — a square representing the opening. The oracle bone forms were more rounded, but the shape settled into the clean square seen today.

## As a Standalone Word

口 by itself means mouth, but in modern Mandarin it is more common in compounds:

出口 — exit; export (mouth going out)
入口 — entrance; import (mouth going in)
人口 — population (people-mouths)
口语 — spoken language
口音 — accent

## Characters Using 口

The 口 component typically signals a connection to:
- Speech and sound
- Eating and the mouth
- Openings and passages
- Calling out

**Characters containing 口:**

- 说 (shuō) — to speak (contains 口 via 言)
- 吃 (chī) — to eat
- 喝 (hē) — to drink
- 叫 (jiào) — to call; to be named
- 问 (wèn) — to ask
- 唱 (chàng) — to sing
- 吗 (ma) — question particle
- 吧 (ba) — suggestion particle
- 哦 (ō/ó) — oh!
- 和 (hé) — and (contains 口)
- 告 (gào) — to tell; to inform

## Common Confusions

口 — mouth (open square)
日 — sun (square with a middle stroke)
目 — eye (tall rectangle with middle stroke)

口 is smaller and has no internal stroke. If there is a line inside, it is a different component.
