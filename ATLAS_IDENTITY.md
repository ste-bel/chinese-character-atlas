# Chinese Character Atlas — Identity

> The infographic was training wheels. This is the destination.

The Atlas is a **Museum of Living Knowledge**. Opening it should feel like
entering a great museum and then being guided, personally, by a scholar through
the evolution of the Chinese language.

This document is the north star for every visual and experiential decision. When
`DESIGN_SYSTEM.md` says *how*, this says *why* and *to what end*. It outranks the
origin infographic (see `ATLAS_BIBLE.md` §7).

---

## The three strands

The identity is not one of these — it is all three, woven together.

1. **Foundation — the classical museum.**
   Timeless, restrained elegance. Premium typography. Real materials: stone,
   bronze, parchment, cinnabar, and gold. Generous whitespace. Nothing shouts;
   everything is considered. The architecture of a national museum's catalogue.

2. **Scholarship — the living manuscript.**
   Authenticity over ornament: annotations, calligraphy, primary historical
   sources, seal impressions, cultural context. The Atlas shows its working. The
   scholar's voice (the Atlas Note) is a person, not a tooltip.

3. **Emotion — the cinematic reward.**
   Used *sparingly*, as reward, never as wallpaper: a gentle page reveal, the
   ember of the Fire Horse, a calligraphy stroke, an oracle-bone reconstruction.
   Motion earns its place by deepening understanding or wonder — otherwise it is
   cut. Always honour `prefers-reduced-motion`.

---

## Marks & guardians

- **The Fire Horse seal** is the Atlas Logo and its **living guardian** — a
  presence that breathes (a slow ember), never an icon. Round seal only;
  aspect preserved; framed by whitespace.
- **白朗志远** (the full composition) is the **author's authentic signature** —
  a colophon on the About page, never navigation.
- The **gold rule**, the **cinnabar seal-stamp**, and the **◆ diamond** are
  recurring motifs — used as wayfinding, not decoration.

## The keywords

Every page is measured against these. If a change does not serve them, cut it.

**Timeless · Scholarly · Premium · Living · Curious · Human · Museum-quality ·
Interactive only when it enhances understanding.**

---

## What this rules out

- Gimmicks. Animation for its own sake. Decoration without meaning.
- Inauthenticity — never fabricate a glyph, an etymology, or a source to fill a
  layout. An honest gap beats a beautiful lie.
- Uniformity as laziness — a museum stages a bronze and a scroll differently.
  Exhibits should vary with what they hold.
- Density mistaken for richness. Whitespace is a material here.

## The test

Before any page ships, ask the §8 Bible question — *"Would I proudly show this to
someone who owns a first-edition Cihai?"* — and one more:

> Does this feel like being shown the character by a scholar who loves it?

If not, it is not done.
